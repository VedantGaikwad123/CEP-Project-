Maharashtra State Solar Policy

Empowering Farmers Through Solar Energy Solutions

Introduction

The Maharashtra State Solar Policy encourages the installation of solar power systems to help farmers achieve energy self-sufficiency. The policy offers a variety of incentives, including financial support for solar pumps and power plants, to promote sustainable agriculture and reduce energy costs.

Key Features of the Policy

Incentives for Solar Pump Installations

The policy provides financial support for the installation of solar irrigation pumps, reducing farmers' reliance on grid and diesel-based energy sources.

Solar Power for Agriculture

Farmers can install solar power plants on their land to power irrigation systems and other farm operations, while surplus energy can be sold to the grid through net metering.

Subsidies and Financial Assistance

The government offers subsidies and low-interest loans to make solar installations affordable for farmers, facilitating the adoption of solar power solutions.

Benefits for Farmers

  • Reduced energy costs by switching to solar power for irrigation and farm activities.
  • Energy independence through solar-powered pumps and plants.
  • Additional income through surplus energy sales under the net metering scheme.
  • Contributing to a cleaner environment by reducing the dependency on fossil fuels.
